Our Strategy

Our objective is to achieve continued success in our chosen markets, delivering a safe, reliable, timely, good value and high-quality experience for all our customers in a way that minimises our impact on the environment. We measure our success through the quality of our service, as seen by our customers, which should result in delivering sustained and profitable growth for the benefit of all our stakeholders.

Our Strategic Pillars

Quality Assets

We operate a modern fleet of multi-purpose ferries and LoLo container vessels between the Republic of Ireland and Britain and Continental Europe.

People & Culture

We ensure that all our people are provided with a high level of training and encourage them to engage with the Group’s continuous improvement philosophy.

Prudent Financial Management

We seek investment opportunities which meet our stringent hurdles both in terms of return and risk and promote this policy at all organisational levels.

Safety

We promote and maintain of a strong safety culture across all activities and benefit from the competitive rewards that come from giving safety top priority.

Environment

We take ongoing action and invest continously in the management and reduction of the environmental impacts of our activities, both on and off-shore.

Strategy in Action

W.B. Yeats and refurbishment of Dublin Swift

Investment in the W.B. Yeats is key to the Group’s decision to offer increased year round direct freight sailings to the continent. The Dublin Swift has undergone additional works to increase car deck capacity and will re-enter service in March offering faster crossing times compared to conventional ferries.

Investment in Container Terminals

In our terminal operations in Dublin we have commissioned two remotely operated rubber tyred gantries on one of our container stacks. Whilst improving the working conditions of the operators this also yields operational efficiencies and improves the safety aspects of terminal operations.

Investment in Marketing

We delivered compelling and personalised offers to our customers at relevant times. This approach helped improve our brand awareness and drove increased levels of enquiries to the Irish Ferries website, which generated over 6.1 million visits, and delivered over 85% of bookings transacted in the year.
